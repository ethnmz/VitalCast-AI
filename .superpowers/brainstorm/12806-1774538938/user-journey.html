How should Vita guide the user?

Same data, three different experiences. Which journey feels right?

A

🏃 The Coach

Motivating · Goal-oriented · Weekly check-ins

Vita says: "Hey [Name], your sleep is your #1 problem right now."
→ Shows sleep score, flags it in red
→ Simulator animates: "If sleep stays at 5.5hrs, your CVD risk hits 14% by December"
→ Slider moves to 7hrs: "This one change drops it to 9.8%"
→ Vita sets a weekly goal: "Sleep 7hrs for 5 out of 7 nights this week"
→ "Come back in 2 weeks. I'll check your progress."
✦ Clear wins  ·  Feels achievable  ·  Reason to return
B

🩺 The Doctor's Report

Clinical · Authoritative · Evidence-based

Vita presents findings:
"3 risk factors identified in your data"
→ #1 Critical: HRV stress index 72/100 (Elevated)
→ #2 Watch: Sleep below clinical threshold (5.5hrs avg)
→ #3 Borderline: Resting HR trending upward

→ Clinical impact projected: "Current trajectory increases 10yr CVD risk by 4.2pp"
→ Evidence-based interventions listed with expected outcomes
✦ Technically serious  ·  Impressive to judges  ·  May feel cold to regular users
C

📖 Vita Tells Your Story

Personal · Narrative-driven · Emotional

Vita narrates:
"Here's what your body told me this month..."

→ "Your heart is working harder than it needs to. Your resting HR has been creeping up — that's a sign your body isn't recovering fully."
→ Simulator shows two futures side by side: This path vs. a better path
→ "The good news: one change — just one — could bend this curve. And I know exactly which one."
→ Vita reveals the single highest-impact action
✦ Most emotional  ·  Vita feels alive  ·  Judges remember stories, not dashboards

The shift in all 3: The dashboard becomes evidence, not the destination. Vita leads. Numbers support. Action is the goal.
